## 💡 Inspiration

**"Is this recipe even worth starting?"**

That question stopped us from cooking far too often. Not because we didn't want to eat well, but because every recipe felt like a gamble filled with unknowns:

- _Am I even going to like this?_
- _Will I waste ingredients I can't afford to replace?_
- _Do I have the time and energy to finish this today?_
- _What if I'm not in the mood by the time it's done?_
- _Can I make this for multiple meals and actually reheat it?_

For people with **ADHD, depression, anxiety, chronic pain, and executive function challenges**, these aren't small concerns—they're barriers that turn cooking from "what should I make?" into "I guess I'm not eating tonight."

Traditional recipe apps show you beautiful photos and ingredient lists, but they don't answer the questions that actually matter when your brain is already exhausted from decision fatigue. They assume you have unlimited executive function to evaluate recipes, unlimited energy to course-correct if you choose wrong, and unlimited money to waste on ingredients for meals you'll never finish.

**We built this app because we were tired of that gamble.**

Our team—all of us navigating executive dysfunction in different ways—realized we needed an app that adapts to where we are _right now_: our energy level, our mood, our time constraints, our meal prep realities. Not just "accessible features," but **a fundamentally different approach to recipe discovery** that reduces the cognitive load of deciding what to cook.

Because cooking shouldn't require you to be at your best to eat well. And you shouldn't need perfect executive function to answer "what's for dinner?"
