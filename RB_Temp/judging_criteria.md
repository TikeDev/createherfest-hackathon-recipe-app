**How Projects Are Judged**

All submissions scored 0-100 across five criteria. Focus on what you do best—you don't need perfection in every area.

🎯 **Key:** Working demo + cited sources + clear problem = strong foundation

**Projects will be evaluated based on:**

## Judging Criteria - 2026

| Criterion | Weight | What Judges Look For | Project Context | Quick Self-Check |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Clarity** | 25% | Problem statement (card) is crisp, Success Test is observable, demo is clear and reproducible | **Presentation & Iteration**: Clear communication of solution **Technical Execution**: Documentation quality | ✅ Can I explain my problem in 10 seconds? |
| **Proof** | 25% | Demo passes from clean start, Evidence Log is linked, sources are cited | **Technical Execution**: Functionality and code quality **Real-World Feasibility**: Evidence of working solution beyond theory | ✅ Does my demo run without help? Do I cite claims? |
| **Usability** | 20% | 3-Line Pitch is clear, captions/alt text present, grade-8 readability | **Gender-Inclusive Innovation**: Accessible design reduces systemic barriers & **Presentation & Iteration**: Communication to diverse audiences | ✅ Can a non-technical friend understand my pitch? |
| **Rigor** | 20% | README & ethical considerations defined, decisions & next risks defined, at least one risk addressed, tradeoffs explained. | **SDG Impact**: Documented alignment with measurable goals + **Ethical Tech Development**: Bias, privacy, exclusion mitigation & **Real-World Feasibility**: Deployment thinking | ✅ Did I document WHY I made key choices? |
| **Polish** | 10% | Timeboxed scope, tidy repo/files, no broken links, clean file structure, ethical considerations documented | **Technical Execution**: Code quality and professionalism **Ethical Tech Development**: Ethics integrated throughout | ✅ Would I be proud to share this link publicly? |

- **How to Maximize Your Score**
    
    **Before You Submit:**
    
    ✅ Test your demo from a clean start (incognito browser or fresh codespace)
    
    ✅ Run your video with captions off—are visuals clear without audio?
    
    ✅ Have someone else read your README—can they set up in under 5 minutes?
    
    ✅ Search your repo for "TODO" or "FIX"—clean them up or document as "Known Issues"
    
    ✅ Check all links in your Devpost, README, and Evidence Log
    
    **Quick Wins for Each Criterion:**
    
    **Clarity:** Add your 4-Line Problem Frame to the top of your README
    
    **Proof:** Create Evidence Log table with 3 cited sources
    
    **Usability:** Run your copy through Hemingway Editor (aim for grade 8)
    
    **Rigor:** Document 1 thing you tried that didn't work and why
    
    **Polish:** Add a .env.example file and remove any hardcoded secrets
    
    💡 **Pro tip:** Use the [Submission Checklist](https://www.notion.so/2f51ac87fe5780a1a562f12a021dce5a?pvs=21) to verify all requirements before deadline.

- **Common Questions**
    
    **Q: Do I need to score high in every category?**
    
    No. You can excel in 3 areas and meet baseline in the others. Focus on your strengths.
    
    **Q: What if my demo breaks before judging?**
    
    Judges will use your demo video and fallback documentation. That's why we require both.
    
    **Q: How much does the pitch matter vs. technical execution?**
    
    Communication (Clarity + Usability + Polish) = 55%. Technical depth (Proof + Rigor) = 45%. Both matter.
    
    **Q: Will I get feedback even if I don't win?**
    
    Yes! All participants receive written feedback from judges.
